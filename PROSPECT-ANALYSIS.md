## Company Profile

### Overview
M-KOPA Holdings Limited is a London-headquartered, Africa-operating fintech founded in 2010 by Jesse Moore (CEO), Chad Larson, and Nick Hughes. The company pioneered Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) asset financing in sub-Saharan Africa, initially with solar home systems, and has progressively expanded into smartphone financing, digital loans, health insurance, device protection, affordable data bundles, and electric bicycles (e-bikes). As of 2025, M-KOPA serves more than **7 million customers** across Kenya, Uganda, South Africa, Nigeria, and Ghana, has deployed **over $2 billion in credit**, and processes **more than 1 million mobile money payments daily**.

### Business Model
M-KOPA's core model is asset-backed PAYG micro-credit. A customer makes a small deposit to receive a smartphone (or other asset). The remaining balance is repaid in daily or weekly micro-instalments via mobile money (M-Pesa in Kenya, MTN Mobile Money in Uganda/Nigeria, etc.). Embedded SIM technology allows M-KOPA to remotely lock the device if payments lapse — dramatically reducing default risk vs. unsecured lending. Once repayment is complete, customers unlock M-KOPA's financial services layer: digital cash loans, health insurance, and device protection. This creates a **recurring revenue flywheel**: smartphone financing is the customer acquisition channel; financial services are the high-margin, capital-light repeat revenue layer.
